chapter 1

A monk lived alone on the edge of a quiet mountain village. Every morning, before the sun rose, he swept the same stone path that led to his small temple. The villagers often wondered why—no one ever used that path.
One day, a young traveler stopped and asked, “Why do you clean a road no one walks on?”
The monk smiled and kept sweeping. “Because I walk on it.”
The traveler frowned. “But that’s not enough reason.”
The monk leaned on his broom. “If I only care for things others see, I become careless when I am alone. But if I care for what is mine to do, whether seen or not, then I stay honest.”
The traveler stayed for a few days, watching. The monk ate simply, spoke little, and worked steadily. Nothing he did seemed important, yet everything felt meaningful.
On the traveler’s last day, he asked, “What is the secret to your peace?”
The monk replied, “I stopped waiting for life to feel important. I just do the next small thing well.”
The traveler left with no grand lesson written down—just the quiet understanding that peace isn’t found in big moments, but built in small, consistent ones.
